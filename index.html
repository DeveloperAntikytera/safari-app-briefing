AI Provider
Comparison

The Safari Guide draws all its answers from the doctor's book and methodology. To make that work at scale without runaway costs, the book needs to be cached. This page explains why, and compares the three providers best suited for the job.

01

The Context

What is a token?
When an AI processes text, it does not read words — it reads tokens. A token is roughly a word, or sometimes just part of a word. "Fertility" is one token. "I am taking CoQ10" is about five tokens. The doctor's book, at approximately 73,000 words, is around 73,000 tokens.
Every time a user sends a message to the Safari Guide, the AI needs context to answer well. Without caching, that means sending the entire book — all 73,000 tokens — alongside every single message. The AI processes it, answers, and discards it. Next message: repeat.
Why the book must be cached
Without caching
Every user message sends the full 73,000-token book to the AI. One user asking 10 questions = 730,000 tokens of book content processed. At 1,000 users asking 10 questions a day, that is 730 million tokens per day — just for the book context, before any actual answers.
With caching
The book is loaded into the AI's memory once. Subsequent messages retrieve it at a fraction of the cost — typically 90% cheaper per token. The user's experience is identical. The cost picture is completely different.
The comparison below evaluates three providers specifically on how they handle this caching — because for Safari App, it is not an optimisation. It is a prerequisite for scalability.

When to Choose Each Provider

Gemini 2.5 Flash

Best when context is large (73k+ tokens). Sending that volume repeatedly creates latency and cost — Gemini's pointer-based caching eliminates both.

OpenAI GPT-4.1 Mini

Best for high-frequency traffic where the cache stays warm automatically. RAG further reduces costs by 93% at scale.

Claude 4.6 Sonnet

Best when tone matters most. Claude consistently delivers the warmest responses — important for an emotionally sensitive wellness journey.

Business
Model

Safari App is a support platform, not a programme. The model is built around a clear hierarchy of contact: AI for everyday guidance, nurses for personalised human support, and doctors for expert opinion on demand.

01

The Nurse Layer

Nurses are the human core of the platform. Not a chatbot, not a doctor — a qualified professional who knows the protocol, understands the journey, and can answer the questions that matter most right now.

Everyday
AI Guide
Available 24/7. Handles habit logging, daily navigation, progress summaries, and general protocol questions grounded in the book.
Good for: "What should I do today?" · "Did I log everything?" · "Explain this chapter."
Human · Core Layer
Nurse
A qualified professional who knows the protocol and the user's specific situation. Responds within 24 hours. Handles personalised, in-depth questions that require real clinical judgement.
Good for: "My results came back — what does this mean for my supplements?" · "I'm feeling X — should I adjust?" · "Is my progress on track?"
On demand
Doctor
A protocol-aligned specialist for cases that require expert medical review. Async, paid per session, and always positioned as complementary to the user's treating clinic.
Good for: second opinions, reviewing test results in depth, or decisions that go beyond the protocol.
Why not just more AI?
AI is excellent at scale and speed. But a fertility journey involves test results, symptoms, and emotional nuance that require a human who is accountable. The nurse provides that — and the AI makes the nurse's workload manageable.
Why not just a doctor?
Doctors are expensive, hard to access, and carry regulatory complexity. Nurses with protocol expertise can answer the majority of meaningful questions faster, more affordably, and with the same personal care — escalating to a doctor only when necessary.
Operational model
Nurse interactions are capped at 2–4 questions per month per subscriber, with a 24-hour async response window. This keeps the workload predictable, the service scalable, and the cost manageable — while ensuring each user gets a meaningful, considered response rather than a quick deflection.

03

The Clinic Relationship

Clinics are a distribution channel, not a customer segment. They benefit from recommending the app and only pay if they want more.

Free to recommend
Any clinic can refer patients at no cost and no setup. They become a trusted referral source — which benefits their patient relationships — without any operational complexity.
Paid dashboard (optional)
Clinics wanting patient visibility — habit adherence, progress, the ability to send guidance — pay for the dashboard. The app works fully without it.
On the doctor layer and clinic conflict
Doctor consultations are always positioned as a complementary view, never a replacement for the treating clinic. The longer path is to invite clinics into the network as consultation providers — turning a potential tension into ecosystem participation.
